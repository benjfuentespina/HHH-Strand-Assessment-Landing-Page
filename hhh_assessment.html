Guidance & Support Resources

üìö Study Tips for Success

Create a Study Schedule - Allocate specific time for each subject. Consistency is more important than intensity.
Form Study Groups - Collaborate with classmates. Teaching others reinforces your own understanding.
Take Active Notes - Don't just copy. Summarize in your own words and ask questions.
Practice Regularly - Especially for STEM subjects. Problem-solving requires hands-on practice.
Seek Help Early - Don't wait until you're struggling. Talk to your teacher as soon as you're confused.

üéØ Academic Preparation

For STEM Students: Master algebra and basic calculus concepts. Lab work and problem sets are essential.
For HUMSS Students: Develop reading comprehension and critical thinking. Practice writing essays and analyzing texts.
For ABM Students: Build strong quantitative skills. Practice accounting, business math, and case studies.
For TVL Students: Develop technical proficiency. Hands-on practice and certifications are key to employability.
For GAS Students: Maintain balanced learning across subjects. Use this year to explore and discover your strengths.

üöÄ Career Exploration

Don't wait until college to explore career options. Attend career fairs, research job descriptions, and interview professionals in fields that interest you. Many schools offer internship opportunities‚Äîtake advantage of them!

Research Colleges: Look into universities offering your strand of choice. Compare programs, facilities, and career placements.
Build Your Portfolio: Depending on your strand, start building projects, writing samples, or a portfolio of technical work.
Develop Soft Skills: Leadership, communication, teamwork, and problem-solving are valued by all employers.
